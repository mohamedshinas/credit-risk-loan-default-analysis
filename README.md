# Credit Risk & Loan Default Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes borrower and loan characteristics using a real-world Lending Club dataset. The objective is to identify patterns influencing lending decisions and credit risk through data preprocessing, exploratory data analysis, and business insight generation.

## Business Problem

Financial institutions face significant challenges in assessing borrower risk and minimizing loan defaults. This project explores how borrower income, credit grades, debt-to-income ratios, and loan characteristics impact lending outcomes.

## Objectives

* Analyze borrower demographics and financial characteristics.
* Identify factors associated with lending risk.
* Examine relationships between income, loan amount, interest rates, and credit grades.
* Generate actionable business recommendations.

## Dataset

Source: Lending Club Loan Dataset

Records: Approximately 5,000–10,000 loan records

Features include:

* Loan Amount
* Annual Income
* Interest Rate
* Credit Grade
* Loan Status
* Debt-to-Income Ratio
* Homeownership
* Loan Purpose

## Key Findings

* Medium-income borrowers dominate the loan portfolio.
* Loan amounts increase with borrower income.
* Lower credit grades are associated with higher interest rates.
* High DTI borrowers exhibit higher lending risk.
* Most loans belong to small and medium loan-size categories.

## Business Recommendations

* Strengthen evaluation of high-risk borrowers.
* Continue risk-based pricing strategies.
* Improve income verification processes.
* Monitor large loan approvals carefully.
* Develop customer-specific lending products.
